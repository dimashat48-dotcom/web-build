INTRODUCING

PUREPLAST

Forget about the old, harmful plastic that we used to have it. No it's time to try the new eco-friendly bioplastic, PURELAST. This bioplastic isn't made from polymer that harm the enviroment, but from an Indonesian traditional food, fermented casssava, a.k.a. "TAPAI SINGKONG." Instead of dissolve into microplastics that harm your own health, this bioplastic will dissolve with the nature since it's biodegradable, or if you just want to get rid of it, dip it in the the water and it will dissappear with the water.

HOW TO MAKE

PUREPLAST

Experience the porcess and the flow in the making of PUREPLAST:

  1. Prepare the tools and materials needed to make fermented cassava (tapai singkong).
  2. Pour 200 ml of water into a pot; do not heat it yet to prevent the flour from clumping.
  3. Add 10 g of tapioca starch, 5 ml of food-grade vegetable glycerol, and 2 ml of white vinegar for the first bioplastic variable, or 2 ml of fermented cassava water for the second variable.
  4. >Heat the pot over low heat while stirring the mixture until it becomes clear and thick.
  5. Prepare an acrylic plate or aluminum tray as a mold once the mixture is translucent and viscous, then pour the mixture onto the tray.
  6. Flatten the mixture on the tray and let it sit for 5–7 days until completely dry.
  7. Peel the bioplastic slowly from the tray once dry to ensure the sheet does not tear.
  8. The bioplastic sheet is ready to be used as a wrapper or a grocery bag.
